# Crafting

Create your own equipment, tools, and items through the various crafting systems in New Dawn.

## Crafting Overview

Crafting in UO allows you to:

- Create equipment for yourself and others
- Make money selling crafted goods
- Be self-sufficient
- Provide services to the community

## Main Crafting Professions

### Blacksmithy

Create metal armor and weapons.

**Key Features:**

- Smelt ore into ingots
- Forge weapons and armor
- Repair metal equipment
- Bulk Order Deeds (BODs)

**Resources:**

- Iron ore/ingots
- Colored ores (if available)
- Forge and anvil access

**Profitability:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

---

### Tailoring

Create cloth and leather armor and clothing.

**Key Features:**

- Craft leather and studded armor
- Make clothing and robes
- Create bandages
- BOD system
- **Tailoring Guild access** at 85.1 skill
- **Dungeon dye system** at GM skill

**Resources:**

- Cloth, leather, hides
- Colored leather (if available)
- Scissors, sewing kit
- Dungeon materials (for dyes)

**Profitability:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

#### Tailoring Guild & Dungeon Dyes

New Dawn features a unique tailoring dye system:

**Joining the Tailoring Guild:**

- Requires **85.1+ Tailoring skill**
- Speak to the Tailoring Guildmaster
- Grants access to special dye crafting

**Crafting Dungeon Dyes:**

1. Reach **100 Tailoring** (GM)
2. Pay guildmaster **10,000 gold**
3. Receive a **Tailors Dye Kit**
4. Farm **250 dungeon-specific materials** (drops from mobs in that dungeon - Patch 0.45)
5. Crush materials and use with the kit
6. Creates one bottle of dye with unique dungeon hue

**Dye Usage:**

- Each bottle has **one use**
- Can dye any cloth item
- Each dungeon has a unique color
- Stackable materials for storage

**Dungeon Dye Materials:**

- Drop from creatures in specific dungeons
- **0.5% drop chance** (Patch 0.34)
- Stackable for easy transport
- Required amount: 250 per dye bottle

## Crafting Quality

### Regular vs Exceptional

- **Regular:** Standard quality items
- **Exceptional:** Better stats, higher value

**Exceptional Chance:**

- Increases with skill
- 100% at GM skill (server dependent)
- Exceptional = better stats

### Material Types

Different materials provide bonuses:

**Metals:**

- Iron (standard)
- Dull Copper, Shadow, Copper, Bronze
- Gold, Agapite, Verite, Valorite

**Leather:**

- Regular
- Spined, Horned, Barbed

**Wood:**

- Regular
- Oak, Ash, Yew, Heartwood, Bloodwood, Frostwood

## Bulk Order Deeds (BODs)

### What are BODs?

Special crafting quests from NPCs:

- Request specific items
- Turn in for rewards
- Earn special resources
- Get rare items

### Types

### Types

- **Small BODs:** Single item type
- **Large BODs:** Multiple item types

### New Dawn BOD System

New Dawn features a **points-based reward system** for BODs:

**Key Features:**

- BODs show their point value when you view them
- BODs show "(complete)" when all requirements are met
- BOD books are **no longer blessed** (can be lost on death)
- BOD timer resets after you turn in a BOD

**Checking BOD Value:**

- Small and Large BODs display point values
- Points determine reward tier
- Complete BODs before turning in for best results

### Tailoring BOD Rewards

#### Small BOD Rewards:

- **1 point:** 80 uncut cloth (hues: 1155, 1164, 1160, 1162)
- **50 points:** 60 uncut cloth (hues: 1173, 1163, 1158, 1157)
- **100 points:** 40 uncut cloth (hues: 1165, 1168, 1166, 1169)
- **150 points:** 20 uncut cloth or low chance sandals (90/10 split, various hues)
- **200 points:** 10 uncut cloth or better chance sandals (80/20 split, various hues)

#### Large BOD Rewards:

- **300 points:** 1 of 4 random stretched hides
- **350 points:** Ore mask dye tier 3 (dull, copper, bronze)
- **400 points:** 1 of 4 random tapestries
- **450 points:** Bear or polar bear rug
- **500 points:** 1 of 4 random tailoring shelves
- **550 points:** Clothing bless deed OR low chance sandals (80/20)
- **600 points:** Ore mask dye tier 2 OR special mask dye tier 2 (80/20, agapite/verite)
- **650 points:** Tailoring powerscroll +5
- **700 points:** Ore mask dye tier 1 OR special mask dye tier 1 (80/20, shadow iron/gold/valorite)

### Rewards

- Runic tools (add magic properties)
- Colored anvils/forges
- Special materials

## Resource Gathering

### Mining

Extract ore for blacksmithing:

- Iron ore (everywhere)
- Colored ores (specific mountains)
- Gems (bonus)
- **3-tile range** (Patch 0.49 - increased from 1 tile)

### Lumberjacking

Harvest wood for carpentry:

- Regular logs (all trees)
- Special woods (specific forests)
- **3-tile range** (increased for convenience)

**Special Wood Types:**

- **Oak:** Rare drop with bark fragments/brilliant amber
- **Ash:** Rare drop with bark fragments/brilliant amber
- **Yew:** Rare drop with bark fragments/brilliant amber
- Tree yield increased but fewer logs per chop
- Logs properly labeled by type

### Skinning/Harvesting

Get leather from creatures:

- Kill animals
- Use bladed weapon on corpse
- Collect leather/hides

### Farming & Gardening

**Cotton & Flax Farming:**

- Wild cotton/flax fields across Britannia
- Young player cotton (young-status only)
- Resource captcha checks during farming
- Renewable resources

**Sheep Farming:**

- Outdoor sheep: Wool every **2 hours**
- Indoor sheep: Wool production based on **hunger level**
  - Well-fed (18-20 food): 1.5 hours
  - Satisfied (10+ food): 2 hours
  - Peckish (5+ food): 6 hours
  - Hungry (2 food): 8 hours
  - Starving: 12 hours
- **Stacked sheep don't produce wool**
- Must maintain proper feeding and space

## Crafting Economics

### Pricing Guide

Consider these factors:

- **Material Cost:** What you paid for resources
- **Time Investment:** How long it took
- **Skill Level:** GM crafters charge more
- **Quality:** Exceptional items worth more
- **Demand:** Popular items = higher prices

### Setting Up Shop

1. **Get a house:** Need storage and workspace
2. **Place vendors:** Hire NPC vendors
3. **Stock items:** Keep variety
4. **Competitive prices:** Research market
5. **Location:** High-traffic areas
